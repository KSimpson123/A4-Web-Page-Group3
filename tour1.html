Northern State Mental Hostpital

Northern State Mental Hospital was built near Sedro-Wolley, Washington in 1909. Although known as one of the better institutions of its time, doctors at the asylum performed experimental “treatments” such as lobotomies, electroshock therapy, and forced sterilization. Patients were often heavily medicated without consent during their stays, and it was common for people suffering from misunderstood conditions like depression, post-menopausal syndrome, or alcoholism to be admitted and kept for extended periods of time. Many never left the facility alive.....
